About Chardham Yatra

Begin your sacred pilgrimage with the Char Dham Yatra, a transformative journey that leads devotees through the most revered spiritual sites in Uttarakhand. The Char Dham, comprising Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath, holds immense significance in Hinduism, symbolizing the path to salvation. These holy shrines, nestled amidst the majestic Himalayas, not only represent the spiritual core of the region but also mark the sources of four sacred rivers – the Yamuna, Ganga, Mandakini, and Alaknanda. A pilgrimage to these sacred places is a lifelong aspiration for devout Hindus seeking spiritual fulfillment.

Initiated by Adi Shankaracharya in the 8th century, the Char Dham Yatra attracts devotees globally.

Char Dham Yatra Tips 2024:

  • Carry a kit of basic emergency medicines.
  • Bottled water during the trip.
  • Consume boiled, cooked, or fried food.
  • Ensure physical and mental fitness, given the trekking involved.
  • Preparatory exercises a month before the yatra.
  • Pack heavy woollens, raincoats, and waterproof shoes.
  • Women are advised to wear comfortable attire.
