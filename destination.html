Overview

Uttrakhand Tour Plan

Uttarakhand is a state in northern India, located in the foothills of the majestic Himalayan mountain range. It shares its borders with Tibet Autonomous Region of China to the north, Nepal to the east, Himachal Pradesh to the west, and Uttar Pradesh to the south. Uttarakhand is renowned for its breathtaking natural beauty, offering a diverse landscape of snow-covered peaks, lush valleys, dense forests, crystal-clear rivers, and cascading waterfalls. The state is often referred to as "Devbhumi" or "Land of the Gods" due to the abundance of pilgrimage sites such as Haridwar, Rishikesh, Kedarnath, and Badrinath.

Formed in November 2000 from the northern part of Uttar Pradesh, Uttarakhand has grown as both a pilgrimage hub and a popular destination for adventure tourism. Traditionally known for its spiritual significance, Uttarakhand has evolved into an all-season destination with a variety of tourism offerings, ranging from trekking in the Himalayas to exploring its rich biodiversity in national parks like Jim Corbett. The state government has actively promoted year-round tourism by developing adventure sports, eco-tourism, and cultural tours, making it a versatile destination for travelers.
